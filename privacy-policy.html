Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 22, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to HealthyLiving ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your data when you use our mobile application.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Profile Information: Age range, biological sex, height, weight, activity level, fitness goals, diet preferences, and equipment access
  • Food Log Data: Food names, nutritional information, meal types, timestamps, and optional food photos
  • Weight Tracking: Weight measurements, dates, and optional notes
  • Workout Data: Completed workouts, exercises, sets, reps, and weights (when logged)
  • Water Intake: Daily water consumption logs
  • API Keys (Optional): Google Gemini API key and/or USDA FoodData Central API key (stored securely in device Keychain)
  • Apple Health Data (Optional): If you enable Apple Health sync, the app reads active calories, step count, and weight entries from Apple Health, and writes weight, height, nutrition, water intake, and workout data to Apple Health. All syncing happens locally on your device. You can enable or disable individual data categories at any time.

2.2 Automatically Collected Information

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system version, app version
  • Usage Data: Features used, app interactions, session duration
  • Crash Reports: Technical data about app crashes or errors (if analytics enabled)

2.3 Photos, Camera Access, and Barcode Scanning

When you use the food recognition or barcode scanning features, we request access to your device's camera and photo library. Photos are:

  • Stored locally on your device only
  • Never uploaded to our servers
  • Only sent to third-party AI services (Google Gemini) if you have configured an API key and explicitly choose to use AI recognition

The app offers two food recognition modes:

  • On-device recognition (Apple Vision): Uses Apple's Vision framework, which runs entirely on your device. No photo or data leaves your device. No API key required.
  • Cloud AI recognition (Google Gemini): Sends a photo to Google's Gemini API for enhanced accuracy on complex or restaurant dishes. Only used when you have configured a Gemini API key and explicitly select this mode. See Section 5.1 for details.

When you use the barcode scanner:

  • The camera is used to detect barcodes on food products
  • No images or video are stored — only the decoded barcode number is used
  • The barcode is sent to Open Food Facts to look up product information (see Section 5)

2.4 Apple Health (HealthKit)

If you enable Apple Health integration in Settings, the app accesses HealthKit data on your device. This includes:

  • Data written: Weight, height, nutrition (calories, protein, carbs, fat, fiber, sugar, sodium), water intake, and completed workouts
  • Data read: Active calories burned, step count, and weight entries from external sources (smart scales, other apps)

All HealthKit data is exchanged locally between HealthyLiving and Apple Health on your device. It is never sent to our servers or any third party. You can enable or disable individual read and write categories at any time in Settings → Apple Health.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information to:

  • Provide personalized nutrition plans based on your profile
  • Generate custom fitness programs tailored to your goals
  • Track your daily nutrition intake and progress toward goals
  • Display analytics and trends about your health data
  • Improve app functionality and user experience
  • Provide customer support when requested

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Local Storage

All your health data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. This includes:

  • Profile information
  • Food logs and nutrition data
  • Weight history
  • Workout logs
  • Photos (stored in app-specific directory)

4.2 Secure Storage

API keys are stored securely in your device's Keychain, which uses encryption to protect sensitive data.

4.3 No Cloud Backup

We do not maintain any cloud servers or databases. Your data stays on your device and is backed up only through your device's iCloud or iTunes backup (if enabled).

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 Optional API Integrations

If you choose to configure API keys, the app may communicate with:

Google Gemini AI

  • Purpose: AI-powered food recognition and nutrition estimation
  • Data Sent: Food photos (converted to base64), food names
  • Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Your Control: Only used when you explicitly select "AI Powered" recognition and have configured an API key

USDA FoodData Central

  • Purpose: Accurate nutritional information from verified database
  • Data Sent: Food search queries, food IDs
  • USDA Privacy: https://www.usda.gov/privacy-policy
  • Your Control: Only used when configured and food logging occurs

Open Food Facts

  • Purpose: Product identification and nutritional data from a crowd-sourced food database
  • Data Sent: Food search queries and/or product barcodes
  • Open Food Facts Privacy: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/privacy
  • Your Control: Only used when you search the Open Food Facts database or scan a barcode. No API key is required. No personal data is sent — only the search term or barcode number.

Apple Health (HealthKit)

  • Purpose: Sync health and fitness data between HealthyLiving and Apple Health
  • Data Exchanged: Weight, height, nutrition, water intake, workouts, active calories, steps (all locally on-device)
  • Apple Privacy: https://www.apple.com/privacy/
  • Your Control: Disabled by default. You must explicitly enable sync in Settings, and can toggle individual data categories on or off at any time.

5.2 No Analytics or Advertising

We do not use third-party analytics services (like Google Analytics or Facebook SDK) and we do not display advertisements.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties, except:

  • When you explicitly use optional third-party features (Gemini AI, USDA, Open Food Facts, Apple Health)
  • If required by law or legal process
  • To protect our rights, property, or safety

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Access Your Data

You can view all your data within the app at any time:

  • Profile: View/edit in Settings → Health Information
  • Food logs: View in Today and Nutrition tabs
  • Weight history: View in Weight tab
  • Workout logs: View in Fitness Plan tab
  • Progress and analytics: View in Status tab

7.2 Export Your Data

You can export all your data in JSON format:

  • Go to Settings → Data Management → Export Data
  • Receive a complete copy of all your health information

7.3 Delete Your Data

You can delete all your data at any time:

  • Go to Settings → Data Management → Delete All Data
  • Confirm deletion (this action is permanent and cannot be undone)
  • All local data, photos, and API keys will be permanently removed

7.4 Opt-Out of Third-Party Services

You can stop using third-party services at any time:

  • Delete your API keys in Settings to disable Gemini AI and USDA
  • Avoid the Open Food Facts and Scan Barcode tabs to stop querying Open Food Facts
  • Turn off Apple Health sync in Settings → Apple Health (or disable individual categories)
  • Use only manual food entry and local features

8. Children's Privacy

HealthyLiving is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us to delete it.

9. International Users

HealthyLiving stores all data locally on your device. If you use optional API features and are located outside the United States, your data may be transmitted to servers in other countries (Google servers for Gemini AI, USDA servers in the US, Open Food Facts servers in France). Apple Health data always stays on your device and is never transmitted externally.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected with a new "Last Updated" date at the top. We will notify you of significant changes through the app or via the app store update notes.

11. Contact Us

12. Legal Compliance

GDPR (European Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Delete your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Export your data
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Stop using API features

CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, you have rights under CCPA:

  • Right to know what data is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt-out of data sales (we do not sell data)
